Graz University of Technology at a Glance

Graz University of Technology is a public research university located in Styria, Austria. It was founded in 1811 by Archduke John of Austria and is the oldest science and technology research and educational institute in Austria.

  • 1811: Founded by Archduke Johann.
  • Graz: Located in Austria's second-largest city.
  • 7 faculties: Research and teaching across 7 faculties.
  • 96 institutes: Broad research and teaching network.

Study Profile

Degree Programmes

  • Bachelor's degree programmes introduce scientific and technical foundations.
  • Master's degree programmes provide subject-specific depth and research orientation.
  • Doctoral programmes lead to a doctorate in technical sciences or natural sciences.

Example Areas

  • Architecture and Civil Engineering Sciences.
  • Computer Science and Biomedical Engineering.
  • Electrical and Information Engineering.
  • Mechanical Engineering and Economic Sciences.
  • Mathematics, Physics, Geodesy, Chemistry, and Process Engineering.

Research Fields of Expertise

Graz University of Technology groups its research activities into five Fields of Expertise. These areas provide a common structure for interdisciplinary cooperation and for research topics with technological and societal relevance.

  • Advanced Materials Science.
  • Human & Biotechnology.
  • Information, Communication & Computing.
  • Mobility & Production.
  • Sustainable Systems.

Why Graz University of Technology?

Technical Focus

  • Clear emphasis on science, engineering, and technology.
  • Research-oriented master's and doctoral education.
  • Strong connection between teaching, laboratories, and applied research.

Research Environment

  • Interdisciplinary research is organised around five Fields of Expertise.
  • Students can connect course work with active research topics.
  • The university supports innovation through laboratories, institutes, and cooperation projects.
